What do I need to do?

Be present

Attendance

Triton College has designated this course as an Extended Remote Learning class (ERL). The class will meet synchronously. This means students MUST be present for each class period. This is a LIVE, web-based synchronous course. Let it be understood that students in this course who miss more than 2 class period sessions will be automatically withdrawn from the class up to the college’s official withdrawal date. Students may be absent for any reason “necessary, including illnesses, personal reasons, death in the family” etc. The instructor will not judge whether one person’s reason for absence is more valid than another person’s reason.

Be on time

Students must submit all major essays by 11:59:00 on the due date. If the essay is submitted after 11:59:00, the paper is considered late. Each paper should be submitted via Blackboard. Please DO NOT send any essays by email. If you need help, please ask. Students must receive at least a D on the multi-sourced research analysis/argument essay in order to be eligible to pass the course.

Tardiness

Because this class meets LIVE, punctuality is required. Tardies will accrue at the rate of 2 tardies=1 absence. Keep in mind that these absences work into your total from the section above. Remember as well that on the days where there are time-sensitive in-class assignments, there will not be extra time to make up missed work. Upon the 5th accrued tardy students will be withdrawn from the class. Any log-ins 10 minutes late or more will be considered absent. Leaving class or logging out before dismissal will be counted as tardy. Please show respect by being on time.

Write

There are 5 formal essays required for this course. All papers must be typed using 12pt font and double spacing.

(1) *Academic Argument (1250-2000 words):

A rhetorical paper where the student persuades the reader to come to a similar conclusion. (topic selections taken from textbook). Essays that fail to reach the word count will receive an automatic F grade. If this assignment is late it will not be accepted. Students who do not pass this essay with a D grade or higher will not pass RHT102.

(2) *Critical Analysis RESEARCH (1250-2000 words):

An analytical essay exploring a concept in literature from selected perspectives (topic selections will be provided) The student should engage with the literary work and also be persuasive in their approach by guiding the reader to come to a similar conclusion. Essays that fail to reach the word count will receive an automatic F grade. If this assignment is late it will not be accepted. Students who do not pass this essay with a D grade or higher will not pass RHT102.

(3) Research Narrative Discourse (800 words):

A rhetorical paper where the student explores and discusses their research process through an experiential lens (research log required as part of grade) If the essay fails to reach the word count the paper will receive an automatic F grade. This assignment will include a research log. Essay Sample

(4) Cause and Effect (1000 words):

A formal rhetorical paper which determines the association between two variables and argues that (b) is the effect of cause (a) or that (a) is the cause of (b) effect or both. (topic choices provided). If the student fails to reach the word count the paper will receive an automatic F grade.

(5) Literary Interpretation (1000 words):

FINAL paper is a student interpretation of a short story (story choices will be provided). No works cited page is required, but it’s a good idea to have one anyway. Essays that fail to reach the word count the paper will receive an automatic F grade.
Essay #5 also serves as the final exam in this course

Plagiarism

Plagiarism refers to copying what someone else has written or taking someone else’s ideas and passing them off as your own. Plagiarism also includes recycling papers the student has written for a previous class. Plagiarism is NOT TOLERATED at Triton College whether intentionally or inadvertently. Violation of this policy in ANY way will result in you receiving an IMMEDIATE "F" IN THE CLASS, and further discipline (a report to the Dean) can be applied as well depending on the severity of the infraction.

Important Information

Course Description:

The writing course sequence develops awareness of the writing process; provides inventional, organizational and editorial strategies; stresses the variety of uses for writing; and emphasizes critical skills in reading, thinking and writing. (Note: grade of "C" or better is an IAI requirement effective summer 1999). IAI designation C1901R.

Course Objectives:

Upon successful completion of this course, the student will be able to write academic research papers with an emphasis on logical, coherent writing skills:

  • Develop the skills, attitudes, and character of a critical thinker.
  • Read analytically
  • Develop a composing technique that exhibits focus, organization, revision, and editing and use appropriate tone and voice for particular audiences.
  • Use standard written English effectively.
  • Demonstrate comprehension of the rhetorical process – engagement as reader, as a writer, and as a thinker.
  • Participate in the literary discourse community of the class to effectively enhance and accommodate various styles of individual and collaborative learning.
  • Appreciate and evaluate the genre of rhetoric and argumentation in and out of the classroom.
  • Utilize academic library materials for research purposes
  • Generate topics from ideas and evidence
  • Analyze audience and adapt message accordingly
  • Develop a specific, focused thesis statement
  • Analyze and incorporating material from appropriate text sources
  • Demonstrate command of Modern Language Association (MLA) formatting
  • Evaluate supporting evidence and blend into research papers
  • Compose 5 course papers, including drafts and revisions. The writing course sequence will include production of two documented, multi-source writings. The papers must have at least 1250 words plus a works cited list in the final version.

Prerequisite:

Complete RHT 101 with a grade of ‘C’ or higher. This may include the AP or CLEP exam. As noted in the catalog, Triton College requires a grade of C or better to pass this course.
